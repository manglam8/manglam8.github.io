03 — Research & Projects

🎓 Coursework — Advanced Computer Vision

Search-and-Rescue via Cost-Oriented POMDP

Course project in Advanced Computer Vision, taught by Prof. John K. Tsotsos (Distinguished Research Professor, Fellow of the Royal Society of Canada). The problem: when should an autonomous agent look versus act? Both have costs. The algorithm optimizes that trade-off using a POMDP framework under real-world resource constraints.

In progress · York University
🎓 Coursework — Fairness & Bias in AI

Neural Network Debiasing on Adult Income Data

Quantified discriminatory bias in income-prediction neural networks using the UCI Adult dataset, measuring fairness metrics including TPR, FPR, and demographic parity across gender and racial groups. Then compared two distinct debiasing methodologies, evaluating the accuracy–fairness trade-off empirically.

Also gave a presentation on InterFair (Majumder et al., EMNLP 2023) — debiasing via natural language feedback.

York University · Winter 2026
⚡ Independent Project · 2024

ML-Based Network Intrusion Detection

Built a supervised classification pipeline to detect anomalous network traffic — framing the intrusion detection problem correctly from the start (most implementations get the feature engineering wrong). Evaluated detection-accuracy vs. false-positive trade-offs under real-time feasibility constraints.

📈 Independent Project · 2024

Stock Price Prediction — ANN Regression

Explored ANN-based regression on historical financial data, benchmarking multiple architectures on error and variance metrics. The interesting part wasn't the model — it was the preprocessing pipeline design and understanding why financial time-series data makes most naive models fail.
